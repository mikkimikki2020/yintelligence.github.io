The photoelectric effect and the discovery of photons

Introduction

General principle of medical imaging

Medical imaging是为临床分析clinical analysis和医疗干预创建身体内部medical intervention的视觉表示的技术和过程

  • Unlike most biomedical signals
    • Not merely （passive） measurements
  • Medical image devices combine
    • Excitation / stimulation of human body
      • Radiation, radioactive tracers, magnetic fields, mechanic vibrations
    • Measurement of the response
      • Transmission / attenuation, photon or proton emission, magnetic field, reflected mechanical wave
    • Computation for image formation
      • Reconstruction of 2D-3D-4D images, from 1D- 2D measurements

Principle of action-reaction: medical imaging often measures response to excitation

Photon-Matter Interactions 光子-物质相互作用

The electromagnetic spectrum

All known frequencies and their linked wavelengths of the known electromagnetic waves, photons or electromagnetic radiation

Photons

Definition

  • A photon is an elementary particle, an elementary bundle of energy
  • The quantum （minimal amount） of all forms of electromagnetic radiation, including light

Energy (Planck postulate)

\[ E = h\nu=\frac{ch}{\lambda} \]

  • ℎ = 6.626 × 10-34J.s (Planck’s constant)
  • c = 3 × 108m/s
  • ν = frequency of the radiation
  • λ = wavelength of the radiation

EM radiation

Basic interactions with matter

Photo-electric effect 光电效应

  1. A high energy incoming X-ray photon knocks out an orbital electron (in figure, a K shell electron being knocked out)

    一个高能入射 X 射线光子敲除一个轨道电子（图中，一个 K 壳电子被敲除）

    The energy of the x ray photon must be equal or greater than the binding energy of the orbital electron

    X射线光子的能量必须等于或大于轨道电子的结合能

  2. The electron knocked out of it’s orbit is a photoelectron

    被撞出轨道的电子是光电子。

    The photoelectron loses its energy as heat in the imaged object

    光电子在成像物体中以热量的形式失去能量

  3. The atom now has an empty orbital electron. The electrons from other orbitals will jump the shells

    原子现在有一个空轨道电子，来自其他轨道的电子将跳过壳层。

    This produces characteristic radiation in the object being imaged （E corresponding to jumps L to K-shell or M to K-shell, etc.）

    这会在被成像物体中产生特征辐射（E对应于L到K-shell或M到K-shell的跳跃等）

  4. This cascade of electrons continues until the atom has filled all it’s empty shells

    这种电子级联一直持续到原子填满它的所有空壳

    In figure, M shell orbital electron ‘jumping orbitals’ filling in L shell, producing characteristic radiation when ‘jumping orbitals’

    图中M壳层轨道电子“跳跃轨道”填充L壳层，“跳跃轨道”时产生特征辐射

Compton effect康普顿效应（或非相干散射incoherent scatter）

  1. An incoming x-ray photon interacts with an outer orbital electron

    传入的 X 射线光子与外轨道电子相互作用

    • Loosely bound, free or dislodged

      松散束缚、自由或移动

  2. The incoming X-ray photon knocks the orbital electron out of its orbit, becoming a recoil electron

    入射的 X 射线光子将轨道电子撞出轨道，成为反冲电子

    • The recoil electron loses energy as heat or creates Bremsstrahlung radiation within the object being imaged

      反冲电子以热量的形式损失能量或在被成像物体内产生轫致辐射

  3. The X-ray photon is deflected in a different direction with less energy (different wavelength = incoherent scatter)

    X射线光子以较少的能量向不同的方向偏转（不同的波长=非相干散射）

    • Energy determined by subtracting the binding energy of the orbital electron f

      通过减去轨道电子 f 的结合能确定的能量

    • The higher the energy of the incoming x ray photon, the smaller angle of deflection （meaning the x-ray photon will continue closer to it’s original path）

      入射 X 射线光子的能量越高，偏转角越小（意味着 X 射线光子将继续靠近其原始路径）

Coherent scattering相干散射 （Rayleigh or Thompson effect）

  1. An incoming x ray photon with low energy interacts with an strongly bound electron （Rayleigh） / loosely bound orbital electron （Thompson）

    具有低能量的入射 x 射线光子与强束缚电子 （Rayleigh）/ 松散束缚轨道电子 （Thompson） 相互作用

    • Energy less than 50 keV / 10 keV

  2. The incoming x ray photon transfers ALL of its energy to the electron

    传入的 X 射线光子将其所有能量转移给电子

    • The incoming photon no longer exists after transferring its energy, the outer orbital electron is excited, but not kicked out of orbit

      入射光子传递能量后不再存在，外轨道电子被激发，但并未被踢出轨道

  3. The outer orbital electron gives off the excess energy （=an X- ray photon） in a different direction

    外轨道电子在不同的方向释放多余的能量（=一个X射线光子）

    • The new x ray photon has the same energy as the incoming x ray photon （same wavelength = coherent scattering）

      新的x射线光子与入射的x射线光子具有相同的能量（相同波长=相干散射）

Interaction of photons with matter: Summary

  • Photo-electric effect
    • Photon is completely absorbed by an atom – An electron is ejected – Some fluorescent radiation is emitted
  • Compton scatter
    • Photon is only partially absorbed
    • An electron and photon （reduced energy） are emitted in 2 different directions
  • Coherent scatter
    • A photon with the same energy is emitted in another direction
  • Different sources of attenuation

The probability for a certain type of interaction may depend on the material （density, atomic number, etc.）, and the energy of the radiation

Impact of radiation

  • Radiation is always kept as low as possible
  • Benefit should outweigh risk
  • Raised attention to young patients due to accumulative risk over time
  • Radiation unit is Gray （Gy = J/kg）
  • Health effect of radiation in Sievert （Sv = J/kg）

X-RAY Imaging

History

  • Discovery of X-rays, Wilhelm Röntgen, 1895
  • Within 1-2 years, first radiology department, Glasgow
  • 1st Nobel Prize in Physics, for discovery of X-rays Wilhelm Röntgen, 1901
  • 3rd Nobel Prize in Physics, for description of radiation phenomena Pierre & Marie Curie, Henri Becquerel, 1903
  • Mathematical framework for image reconstruction （CT）, Johann Radon, 1917

Physical principle

  • X-ray attenuation is determined by different factors

    • Energy of incoming beam 入射光束的能量 (（ ~1/E3）
    • Atomic number of object 物体的原子序数 （ ~Z3）
    • Density of object 物体的密度
    • Size of object 对象的大小
    • Effective atomic number 有效原子序数
      • Soft tissue 软组织 Z=7.5
      • Bone 骨骼 Z = 11

  • When X-rays are transmitted through an object and captured

    当 X 射线穿过物体并被捕获时

    • The differential attenuation of the rays provides the contrast of the image

      光线的不同衰减提供了图像的对比度

    • Mainly Compton and Photo-electric effect

      主要是康普顿和光电效应

Instrumentation

  • Source

  • Low-energy filter 低能耗过滤器

    • Photons which are fully attenuated in patient do not contribute to image contrast, only induce radiation to patient

      在患者体内完全衰减的光子对图像对比度没有贡献，只会对患者产生辐射

  • Collimator

    • Select / filter photons based on angle, as scattered photons reduce image quality （blur, noise）

      根据角度选择/过滤光子，因为散射光子会降低图像质量（模糊、噪声）

  • Detector

Source - X-ray tube

  • Thermionic emission 热电子发射

    • Emission of electrons from a hot metal cathode into a vacuum

      电子从热金属阴极发射到真空中

  • Electrons are ejected and accelerated by strong electric field

    电子被强电场射出并加速

    • Potential difference 30kV - 140 kV

  • Electrons hit target, and produce X-ray photons （Bremsstrahlung）

    电子击中目标，并产生 X 射线光子（Bremsstrahlung）

    • 99% is actually converted to heat

  • Two parameters of X-ray source

    • Number of emitted photons
      • Determined by current of cathode
    • Energy of the emitted photons
      • Determined by potential difference

Detectors

  • Screen-film detector 屏幕胶片探测器

    • Analog system

    • Photographic film （AgBr or AgI）, combined with image intensifier to increase efficiency

      照相胶片（AgBr 或 AgI），结合图像增强器以提高效率

      • Radiographic cassettes 射线照相暗盒

      • Developed, and inverted （high absorption, white)

        显影、倒置（高吸收、白色）

  • Direct radiography 直接射线照相

    • Digital system

    • Flat panel with direct readout 平板直接读数

      • Different technologies exist 存在不同的技术

      • Relying on CCD cameras, X-ray conversion to visible light, or direct conversion to electric signals

        依靠CCD相机，X射线转换为可见光，或直接转换为电信号

Image formation

Image records transmission of x-rays through object \[ I_d(x,y)=I_0\exp(-\int\mu(x,y,z)dl) \] The integral is a line-integral or a projection through object, accumulating attenuation 积分是线积分或穿过物体的投影，累积衰减

  • µ（x,y,z） = X-ray attenuation coefficient

Clinical use

In many cases, first choice of imaging

  • Historically built experience in X-ray image interpretation 历史悠久的 X 射线图像解释经验
  • Less radiation than CT 辐射比 CT 少
  • Fast and cheap 又快又便宜

Lower sensitivity due the projective image formation

  • 2D representation of 3D anatomy 3D 解剖结构的 2D 表示

  • Performs best when examining objects with strongly different attenuation coefficient

    在检查衰减系数差异很大的物体时表现最佳

  • Bone imaging, thoracic imaging 骨成像、胸腔成像

Variants

CT Imaging

History

  • Computed tomography
    • Tomography （τόμος）, refers to imaging by sections, slices
  • Mathematical foundations for CT, Radon,1917
  • Experimental development of technique, A.M. Cormack, 1963
  • First CT-scanner （EMI-scanner）, G. Hounsfield, 1972
  • Nobel prize medicine and physiology, G. Hounsfield & A.M. Cormack, 1979

Physical principle

  • Take large number of X-ray measurements from different angles

    • Rotate source and detector around object in a plane

      在平面上围绕物体旋转源和探测器

  • Compute 3D images

    • Using image reconstruction techniques 图像重建技术

  • The resulting 3D images are maps of the X-ray linear attenuation coefficient μ 生成的 3D 图像是 X 射线线性衰减系数 μ 的图

    • Tomographic images, slices 断层图像、切片

Hounsfield units 亨氏单位 （HU）

Linear transformation of the original linear attenuation coefficient

  • Ratio of linear attenuation coefficient of a voxel to that of water

    体素的线性衰减系数与水的线性衰减系数之比

  • HU value is related to attenuation within that volume （ ~ electron density）

    HU 值与该体积内的衰减有关（~电子密度）

\[ CT(x,y)=1000\frac{\mu(x,y)-\mu_{water}}{\mu_{water}} \]

Hounsfield scale results in （quasi -） quantitative measurements Hounsfield

量表导致（准）定量测量

  • HU can be compared across acquisitions and scanners

    可以在采集和扫描仪之间比较 HU

Instrumentation

A. First generation

  • Pencil beam, one source → one detector
  • Translation and rotation of source and detector

B. Second generation

  • Fan beam, from source to array of detectors

  • Translation and rotation of source and detector, but with less angles

    源和探测器的平移和旋转，但角度较小

C. Third generation

  • Wide fan beam covering entire object
  • No more translations

D. Fourth generation

  • Wide fan beam, stationary detector ring

    宽扇形光束，固定探测器环

  • More scatter

Image formation

  • For an array of detectors

    • Each projection is a line （A in image below）

      每个投影都是一条线（下图中的 A）

  • The concatenation 串联 of all lines

    • The sinogram

  • Lines are back projected in 3D space （B）

    线条在 3D 空间中反向投影（B）

    • Uniform intensity along ray

      沿射线的均匀强度

  • Back projections of all projections are accumulated （C）

    累积所有投影的反投影（C）

    • Blurry version of original image

      原始图像的模糊版本

  • Filtered back projection can remove blur

    过滤反投影可以去除模糊

    • Filtered back projection reconstruction

      滤波反投影重建

Filtered back projection: examples

  • Filtering allows to compensate blurring 过滤允许补偿模糊

    • Convolution filter to remove blurring 卷积过滤器去除模糊

  • Analytical technique 分析技术

    • Perfect reconstruction in case of infinite projections 在无限投影的情况下完美重建

  • Streak artifacts 连胜神器

    • Consequence of using finite amount of projections 使用有限数量的预测的结果
    • A large amount of projections needed in practice 实践中需要大量的预测

  • Iterative reconstruction 迭代重建

    • Solves discrete problem （per pixel / voxel）

      解决离散问题（每像素/体素）

    • Low amount of artifacts and noise 少量的伪像和噪音

    • Becoming increasingly popular 来越受欢迎

Clinical use

First choice for detailed anatomical imaging 详细解剖成像的首选

  • Excellent geometric accuracy and high resolution

    出色的几何精度和高分辨率

Better soft tissue differentiation than X-ray 比 X 射线更好的软组织分化

  • Not nearly as good as MRI 不如 MRI

  • Liver, lung tissue, fat, muscle, .. 肝脏、肺组织、脂肪、肌肉、..

  • Further improvement of contrast 进一步提高对比度

    • Intravenous administration of contrast material

      进一步提高对比度

    • Iodine-based liquid with high attenuation

      高衰减的碘基液体

Better sensitivity for detecting abnormalities 更好的灵敏度检测异常

  • Improved contrast 改进的对比度
  • 3D representation vs. projection imaging 3D 表示与投影成像

Variants

Dual energy CT

  • Use X-rays of two different energy spectra 使用两种不同能谱的 X 射线
    • High energy beam 高能光束 140 kVp
    • Low energy beam 低能量光束 80 kVp
  • Differentiate better between different materials 区分不同材料之间更好
    • Differential attenuation at different energy provides additional contrast 不同能量下的差分衰减提供额外对比度
    • Accurate identification of material, e.g. injected iodine contrast material 准确的材料识别，例如 注射碘造影剂

Radioactive Decay

Introduction to radioactivity

Types of nuclear radiation

Nuclear Radiation Types - Alpha

An alpha particle consists of two protons and two neutrons

α粒子由两个质子和两个中子组成

  • Very large on an atomic scale 在原子尺度上非常大
  • Positively charged 带正电

Penetration in materials 材料渗透

  • Outside the body, an alpha emitter is not a hazard unless it is on the skin 在体外，除非在皮肤上，否则 alpha 发射器不会造成危害

  • Inside the body, an alpha emitter is a bigger hazard if it deposits its energy in sensitive tissue

    在身体内部，如果阿尔法发射器将能量储存在敏感组织中，它的危害更大

Nuclear Radiation Types - Beta

A beta particle is a charged electron β粒子是带电的电子

  • Has the size and weight of an electron 具有电子的大小和重量

  • Can be positively or negatively charged （beta+ an beta-）

    可以带正电或带负电（beta+ 和 beta-）

Penetration in materials 材料渗透

  • At low energies, a beta particle is not very penetrating

    • stopped by the outer layer of skin or a piece of paper

    在低能量下，β 粒子的穿透力不是很强 - 被皮肤外层或一张纸阻止

  • At higher energies, a beta particle may penetrate the skin

    在较高能量下，β 粒子可能会穿透皮肤

  • Inside the body, a beta particle is not as hazardous because it is not as big 在体内，β 粒子并不那么危险，因为它没有那么大

  • Because it is not as big, it travels farther, interacting with more tissue （but each small piece of tissue gets less energy deposited）

    因为它没有那么大，所以它传播得更远，与更多的组织相互作用（但每一小块组织沉积的能量都更少）

Nuclear Radiation Types - Gamma

Gamma radiation is electromagnetic radiation or photons 伽马辐射是电磁辐射或光子

  • Has no weight
  • Has no charge

Penetration in materials

  • At low energies, a photon can be stopped by a very thin （almost flexible）layer of lead or several centimetres of tissue

    在低能量下，光子可以被非常薄（几乎灵活）的铅层或几厘米的组织阻止

  • At higher energies, inches of lead might be necessary to stop a photon and they can pass right through a human

    在更高的能量下，可能需要几英寸的铅来阻止光子，并且它们可以直接穿过人类

Particles emitted by radioactive decay

  • Alpha-particles: strong ionisation, very short penetration
    • Not usable for detection, do not escape body
  • Beta-particles （electrons）: ionising, short range in tissue
    • Only used in therapy for their destructive nature
  • （X or） Gamma Photons: weakly ionising, long range
    • High penetration probability in tissue
    • Can be used for detection and imaging

SPECT Imaging

Medical imaging - Overview

SPECT - Nuclear medicine imaging

  • Single-Photon Emission Computed Tomography

    单光子发射计算机断层扫描

  • Morphological imaging 形态学成像（MR / CT）

    • Anatomical information （~proton density, ~electron density）

      解剖信息（质子密度，电子密度）

    • High spatial resolution （~ 1 mm）

      高空间分辨率（~ 1 毫米）

  • Functional imaging 功能成像 （PET, SPECT）

    • Image functional processes in body

      身体中的图像功能过程

    • Limited resolution (~ 5-12 mm for humans）

      分辨率有限（人类约为 5-12 毫米）

  • Why image functional processes? 为什么要进行图像功能处理？

    • Complementary information allowing better understanding

      补充信息可以更好地理解

    • Functional changes can appear earlier than anatomical

      功能变化可以比解剖更早出现

      • Onset of diseases processes 疾病过程的发作
      • Response to treatment 对治疗的反应

Physical principle

  • Injection of radioactive tracer 注射放射性示踪剂

    • Radiopharmaceutical 放射性药物

      • Active molecule bound to radioactive agent

        与放射性试剂结合的活性分子

  • Tracer accumulates and decays locally

    示踪剂在本地累积和衰减

    • Higher concentration of tracer where it accumulates

      较高浓度的示踪剂积聚处

  • Single photon is emitted （Gamma ray!)）

    单光子发射（伽玛射线！）

    • Captured along emission line in detector

      沿探测器中的发射线捕获

  • Contributions from many angles are summed 总结了多角度的贡献

    • Allows generation of 3D image 允许生成 3D 图像

Radiopharmaceutical

  • Goal

    • To visualise a molecule that shows a specific metabolic or physiological process in the body

      可视化显示体内特定代谢或生理过程的分子

  • Labelling

    • Molecule is made visible by marking / coupling it with a radioactive isotopes, which decays

      分子通过标记/偶联放射性同位素而可见，后者会衰变

  • Criteria

    • No change in chemical properties of the molecule

      分子的化学性质没有变化

    • Used in very low concentrations

      以非常低的浓度使用

    • Strong binding between molecule and isotope

      分子与同位素之间的强结合

Nuclear medicine - Workflow

Instrumentation

Measure projection of radioactive molecules along each line

测量放射性分子沿每条线的投影

Image acquisition

Image formation

  • Measurements acquired over different angles
    • Sinogram as in CT
  • Use of filtered back projection 滤波反投影
    • Reconstruction of 3D images

Scatter and attenuation

  • Attenuation
    • Gamma photons not detected due to additional interactions the body 由于身体的额外相互作用，未检测到伽马光子
      • Photo-electric effect
      • Compton effect
  • Scatter
    • Generated photons scattered in object due to interactions 由于相互作用而产生的散射在物体中的光子
    • Reach detector along different line, and lead to wrong contribution during reconstruction 沿着不同的路线到达检测器，并在重建过程中导致错误的贡献

Clinical use of SPECT

  • Measurement of function or activity of an organ 器官功能或活动的测量

    • Poor anatomical description 糟糕的解剖描述

  • Popular uses of SPECT SPECT 的流行用途

    • Closely related to available tracers that emit gamma’s 与发射伽马的可用示踪剂密切相关

  • Cardiac imaging 心脏成像

    • Detect infarction of heart muscle 检测心肌梗塞

  • Bone scintigraphy 骨显像

    • 2D planar image 二维平面图像

    • Bone metabolism, remodelling or healing, metastases

      骨代谢、重塑或愈合、转移

PET Imaging

SPECT versus PET

Injection of radioactive labelled molecule

  • Single photon （gamma）
  • Positron emitter （Beta+）

Physical principle

Positron Emission Tomography

  • Label molecule 标签分子
    • Radioactive positron emitter 放射性正电子发射器
  • Positron will annihilate 正电子会湮灭
    • Two photons in opposite direction 两个光子方向相反
  • Capture the gamma photon 捕获伽马光子
    • Reconstruct a 3D image 重建 3D 图像

Workflow

Instrumentation

PET is a full ring 满环

  • Scanner does not rotate 扫描仪不旋转

Detections always occur in pairs 检测总是成对发生

  • Less scatter 减少分散

  • No need for collimator （as in SPECT）

    无需准直器（如 SPECT）

  • Detector pairs can be organized according to angle and radial distance

    可以根据角度和径向距离组织探测器对

Sinogram is continuously updated at all angles 全方位持续更新

  • Longer scan times → more detections

    更长的扫描时间 → 更多的检测

Image formation

  • PET directly measures all angles of sinogram

    PET 直接测量正弦图的所有角度

    • SPECT angle per angle

  • Reorganising acquisition data 重组采集数据

    • Allows to use filtered back projection 允许使用过滤反投影
    • Iterative reconstruction 迭代重建
      • Iteratively optimize the value for a voxel 迭代优化体素的值
      • More suited for PET data 更适合PET数据

Clinical use

Popular functional modality in oncology 肿瘤学中流行的功能模式

  • Excellent sensitivity for detection, staging and monitoring of tumours

    对肿瘤的检测、分期和监测具有出色的灵敏度

Often combined with CT

  • Underlying anatomical information enhances interpretation

    潜在的解剖信息增强了解释

Compared to SPECT

  • Better sensitivity 更好的灵敏度
  • Higher resolution 更高分辨率

Popular tracer示踪剂: FDG

  • Glucose analogon 葡萄糖类似物
  • Will accumulate where metabolic activity takes place 会在代谢活动发生的地方积累

Expensive modality

  • Time of acquisition 获取时间
  • Radiopharmaceutical 放射性药物

Electron spin and precession

MR Imaging

  • Magnetic Resonance Imaging - 磁共振成像 （MRI）

    • Previously Nuclear Magnetic Resonance （Imaging）

      以前的核磁共振（成像）

  • Negative connotations associated with the word nuclear

    与“核”这个词相关的负面含义

    • Many patients thought that the exam would expose them to radiation 许多患者认为检查会使他们暴露在辐射中

    • Name progressively changed from 1970’s

      名称从 1970 年代开始逐步更改

  • MRI is non-invasive, harmless MRI 是非侵入性，无害

    • Involves magnetic fields, no x-rays or radioactive decay

      涉及磁场，无 X 射线或放射性衰变

    • Involves the resonance of the atom core （nucleus）

      涉及原子核（原子核）的共振

History

  • The concept of magnetic field and rotating magnetic field, Faraday, 1855; Nikola Tesla, 1882
  • Electrons have an angular momentum or spin （nuclei also）, Compton, Pauli, Dirac, 1920-1930
  • First observation of the Nuclear Magnetic Resonance （NMR）, Bloch and Purcell, 1947; Nobel Prize in Physics, 1952
  • Start of NMR Imaging, P. Lauterbur （test tube） and R. Damadian （humans）, 1970
  • Improvements to MR imaging, P. Mansfield, 1976
  • MRI as a diagnostic tool, Nobel Prize for Medicine and Physiology – Lauterbur & Mansfield, 2003

Physical Principle

  • The composition of the human body is primarily fat and water

    • Fat and water have many hydrogen atoms

      脂肪和水有许多氢原子

    • 63% of human body is hydrogen atoms

      人体63%是氢原子

  • Hydrogen nuclei 1H have an NMR signal 氢核 1H 有核磁共振信号

    • MRI uses hydrogen because it has only one proton

      MRI 使用氢，因为它只有一个质子

    • Aligns easily with the MRI magnet （external field）

      易于与 MRI 磁铁对齐（外场）

  • The hydrogen atom’s proton, possesses a property called spin

    氢原子的质子，具有称为自旋的特性

    • A small magnetic field 小磁场
    • Will cause the nucleus to produce an NMR signal 会导致核产生核磁共振信号

  • MRI is basically a large magnet MRI基本上是一个大磁铁

    • Patients lie within magnetic field 病人躺在磁场内

  • Atomic nuclei have a magnetic moment 原子核具有磁矩

    • Moments tend to become aligned to applied field

      时刻倾向于与应用领域对齐

    • Creates local magnetization m（x,y,z）

      创建局部磁化 m（x,y,z）

      • A tissue property 组织属性

  • MRI制作 m(x,y,z) 的地图

Image formation

  • The magnetization is excited locally 局部激发磁化

    • Using an RF excitation 使用射频激励

  • Magnetization emits energy at a resonant frequency 磁化以共振频率发射能量

    • Frequency is proportional to magnetic field

      频率与磁场成正比

    • Measure frequency, and relate to local magnetisation

      测量频率，并与局部磁化相关

  • Spatially varying magnetic field 空间变化的磁场

    • Measurements combining different phases and frequencies allow to compose a 3D map

      结合不同相位和频率的测量允许组成 3D 地图

\[ \omega = \lambda B \]

Instrumentation

  • Primary magnet 初级磁铁

    • Creates the magnetic field by coiling electrical wire and running a current through the wire

      通过缠绕电线并使电流通过电线来产生磁场

  • Gradient coil or magnets 梯度线圈或磁铁

    • Allow for the magnetic field to be altered 允许改变磁场
    • Allow image slices of the body to be created 允许创建身体的图像切片

  • RF coil 射频线圈

    • Emits the radiofrequency pulse allowing for the alignment of the protons 发射射频脉冲允许质子对齐

    • Used as antenna’s to receive signals sent back from the aligning protons （relaxation）

      用作天线以接收从对准质子发回的信号（松弛）

Clinical use

  • Non-invasive and harmless method 无创无害的方法
    • No radiation or radioactive decay 无辐射或放射性衰变
  • Many MRI sequences exist 存在许多 MRI 序列
    • radiofrequency pulses and gradients that result in images with particular appearance 产生具有特殊外观的图像的射频脉冲和梯度
  • Large number of types of MRI, and clinical applications 大量类型的MRI，以及临床应用

US Imaging

  • Medical ultrasound imaging 医学超声成像

    • Also called （diagnostic） sonography or ultrasonography

      也称为（诊断）超声检查或超声检查

    • Diagnostic imaging technique based on the application of ultrasound

      基于超声应用的诊断成像技术

  • Ultrasound 超声波

    • Sound waves with frequencies higher than the upper audible limit of human hearing （>20kHz）

      频率高于人类听觉听觉上限的声波 （>20kHz）

    • For imaging, frequencies > 2MHz are used

      对于成像，使用频率 > 2MHz

    • No different from normal （audible） sound in its physical properties

      物理特性与正常（可听）声音没有区别

  • Most known for prenatal imaging of foetus 最著名的是胎儿的产前成像

Physical principle

  • Inspired by nature

    • Echo of sound waves in the mountains
    • Natural sonar of whales

  • Tissue is composed of point scatters 组织由点散射组成

    • US-waves scatter because of changes in acoustic impedance声阻抗
    • Changes in density or compressibility 密度或可压缩性的变化

  • Reflected wave is recorded

    • Using ultrasound probe （detector）
    • Recording of amplitude, phase and frequency of received signal

  • Conventional US imaging is based on longitudinal pressure wave

    传统的美国成像基于纵向压力波

    • Change of density & pressure in the direction of the propagation 沿传播方向的密度和压力变化
    • Like motion of air molecules for sound waves 像空气分子的声波运动

  • Similar properties as EM waves EM 波相似的特性

    • Wavelength and frequency 波长和频率
    • Propagation speed depends on medium 传播速度取决于介质

  • Purely mechanical phenomena 纯机械现象

    • No radiation, no photons 无辐射，无光子
    • Only mechanical energy 只有机械能

Different types of scattering

Diffusive scattering （most common in US）

  • scattering object < US
  • wavelength backscattered power ~ f4 = Rayleigh scattering
  • Example= red blood cells

Specular reflection

  • scattering object > US
  • wavelength Example = vessel wall, bone

Acoustic Impedance 声阻抗

Measure of opposition or resistance that a system presents to the acoustic flow resulting of an acoustic pressure applied to the system 系统对施加于系统的声压所产生的声流的阻力或阻力的量度 \[ Z = ρ · c \]

Instrumentation

  • The piezoelectric effect 压电效应

    • Relation between a mechanical stress and an electrical voltage in solids 固体中机械应力和电压之间的关系

  • Reversible phenomena 可逆现象

    • Mechanical stress will generate a voltage

      机械应力会产生电压

    • Applied voltage will change the shape of the solid by a small amount （up to a 4% change in volume）

      施加的电压会少量改变固体的形状（体积变化高达 4%）

  • Only occurs in some non-conductive materials 只发生在一些非导电材料中

Transducer creates US waves via piezo-electric effect= Conversion of electrical to acoustic energy and vice versa → Used for both transmission and detection

换能器通过压电效应产生美国波=电能与声能的转换，反之亦然→ 用于传输和检测

  • US probe composed of array (matrix) of transducer elements

    US 探头由换能器元件阵列（矩阵）组成

    • Elements are triggered in a certain sequence

      元素按特定顺序触发

    • Suitable delays cause formation of a wave front, a （focussed） beam 适当的延迟导致形成波前，（聚焦）光束

  • Response to a wave front is measured 对波前的响应被测量

    • Interpreted, taking into account to given delays

      解释，考虑到给定的延误

Clinical use

It is a non-invasive and harmless method with several advantages

  • Portable and relatively cheap 便携且相对便宜
  • Real-time 2-D and 3-D images at high frame rates 高帧率下的实时 2-D 和 3-D 图像
  • Tissue and blood velocity estimation （Doppler echo） 组织和血液速度估计（多普勒回波）

It is omnipresent in the clinic

  • Imaging in obstetrics and gynaecology 产科和妇科成像
  • Cardiovascular imaging （heart and vessels） 心血管成像（心脏和血管）
  • Abdominal imaging （liver and other organs） 腹部成像（肝脏和其他器官）
