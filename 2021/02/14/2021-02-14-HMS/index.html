An introduction to hospital IT, electronic medical and patient records, eHealth and emerging technologies

Data are symbols or observations reflecting differences in the world.

  • Example = 250.00 (Note: data is the plural of datum)

Information is data with meaning.

  • Example = ICD-9 code of 250.00 means type 2 diabetes

Knowledge is information that is justifiably believed to be true.

  • Example = obese patients are more likely to develop type 2 diabetes

Patient is free to choose

  • The GP, specialist or hospital he prefers
  • Whether he changes doctor or institute
  • …

Not included in this scheme

  • Billing and reimbursement
  • Social security and health insurance
  • Screening
  • …

In an ideal world 1:09:57

An efficient and effective system should

  1. Maintain and share information between all stakeholders

    • Demographics, allergies, prior conditions

    • Blood pressure, taken medication, family conditions

    • Prior imaging, laboratory analysis, biopsy results

    • ….

    Health information systems

  2. Maximally exploit that prior health data

    • To improve patient diagnosis and outcomes
    • Optimize regulations and reimbursement rules
    • Discover new relations between conditions-drugs-analyses
    • …

    Clinical research and decision support

Current status 1:11:52

Why is Health informatics challenging? 1:24:35

Huge amounts of data

  • Text files, protocols, lab results

  • Images, biological tissues, DNA sequences

    No central storage system

Health data challenges

  • Privacy laws limit the exchange of data

  • Issues with security, authorization, authentication

    Who can access what?

Why is mining medical data challenging? 1:26:12

Complex modelling task

  • Healthcare processes involve many actors

  • Vary across institutions and change constantly

    Systems should be integrated and evolve flexibly over time

Difficulty of data interpretation

  • Larger semantic gap

  • With other industries, “data” and “information” are much closer

    • Banking data
      • “$100.50” is close to “an account balance of $100.50”
      • Little room for a different interpretation
    • Healthcare data
      • More subjective: “I feel sick”, difficult to measure

    Need for conventions and standardization

Establishing a health information system (HIS) 1:27:42

Need for strategy, architectures, education and research in HIS

  1. Shift from paper-based to computer-based processing and storage
  2. Shift from institution-centered towards regional and global HIS
  3. Inclusion of patients and health consumers
  4. Use of HIS data for health care planning and clinical and epidemiological research
  5. Shift from focusing on technical HIS problems to strategic information management
  6. Inclusion of new types of data
  7. Inclusion of new technologies

Different concepts and interpretations 1:29:50

Electronic Medical Record (EMR) 1:38:34

Purposes

  • Collecting relevant data
  • Reporting
  • Management of medical data
  • Administrative management
  • Attestation
  • Correspondence
  • …

What should be in the EMR?

STEP 1: Simplifying in a ridiculous way

  • One doctor only, for instance a general practitioner

  • Not part of any hospital, center, …

  • No shared information, …

  • Patients don’t visit multiple doctors

  • …

  • Doctor keeps track of patients and encounters with the patient

  • 1 patient + 1 doctor + 1 encounter = 1 “case”

  • EMR = System to store cases, but what is a case?

Defining the elements of a “case”: 1 patient, 1 doctor, 1 encounter

  • Invent a very simple scenario of a patient with a simple problem.
  • Invent a patient with multiple chronic diseases.
  • What data does the doctor need to document these two cases?
  • What is the same and what is different for both cases and WHY is it?

STEP 2: Cluster 1:43:42

Cluster according to SOAP

  • 1 patient + 1 physician + 1 contact = 1 case
    • Subjective element
      • Patient's complaint
    • Objective measurement
      • From investigation, blood work, imaging
    • Assessment
      • E.g. renal failure
    • Planning
      • 30mg Abc /day, X-ray ordered
Soap in a nutshell 1:49:04
Subjective
  • The chief complaint (CC) or the history of present illness (HPI).
  • This is the main reason of the patient’s visit
  • OLD CARTS
    • Onset
    • Location
    • Duration
    • Character
    • Alleviating / Aggravating factors
    • Radiation
    • Temporal Pattern
    • Severity
Objective

Information gathered by the doctor, through observation or measurement, including:

  • Vital signs (height, weight, temperature, BP, …)
  • Physical exam
  • Laboratory tests
  • Prescriptions
Assessment

This is the medical diagnosis

  • Assessment of the situation, even if obvious
  • Includes possible and objective statements
  • Does not repeat the subjective and objective data, but key points reaching a conclusion
Plan

What the doctor will do treat the patient’s concerns and based on the assessments:

  • Ordering lab tests, radiological work-ups
  • Referrals, discharge, future appointments, etc.
  • Prescriptions, monitoring
  • Numbered by severity and urgency
How does a real SOAP look like?

Electronic health record (EHR)

Also wrongly referred to as

  • Electronic medical record (EMR), USA
  • Electronisch medisch dossier (EMD->hospital), Globaal medisch dossier (GMD->GP), BE

Definition

  • The systematized collection of patient and population electronically-stored health information in a digital format (Wikipedia)
  • An electronic version of a patients medical history, that is maintained by the provider over time, and may include all of the key administrative clinical data relevant to that person’s care under a particular provider, including demographics, progress notes, problems, medications, past medical history, radiology reports… (Medicare, USA)

In practice

  • Work in progress, reality is yet to meet our ambitions

The EHR is more than “simple” cases

We defined earlier an EMR as a system to store cases: 1 patient, 1 doctor, 1 encounter.

This approach does not reflect typical healthcare organization

  • Patient relationships
  • Patient’s history
  • Multiple treating physicians
  • …

EHR = EMR that conforms to nationally recognized interoperability standards and that can be created, managed, and consulted by authorized clinicians and staff across more than one health care organization

The EHR is typically part of a practice management system (PMS)

Recall

An Electronic Health Record could be structured on a case basis:

  • One patient + one doctor + one visit = 1 case

Information could be structured according to the “SOAP” principle

  • Subjective
  • Objective
  • Assessment
  • Plan

Even if we consider an individual doctor that does not share any information with any organisation, free text of each of the SOAP fields is far from optimal …
