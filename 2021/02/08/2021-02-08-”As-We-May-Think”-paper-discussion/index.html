”As We May Think” paper discussion

VUB 4019862ANR Web Technologies

Background + Video + Slide + Questions + Solution

Try to answer the following questions about the paper ”As We May Think” from Vannevar Bush(1945).

Note that for many of these questions there is no single ”correct” answer. This also means thatyou should motivate your answers! The exam is oral, meaning you can be asked more in-depthquestions!

Ideas

Question 1: If you had to select a particular idea from the paper that you liked best -what would it be? How would you explain the idea to a non-expert?

  • Dry photography 干摄影

  • Walnut camera 胡桃木相机

  • Speech to text, text to speech 语音转文字，文字转语音

  • Memex (Memory Expander 内存扩展器)

  • Scanner 扫描仪 (part of the Memex)

  • Smaller storage medium 更小的存储介质

  • Interfacing the human nerve system directly with computers, for both input as output

    将人类神经系统直接与计算机接口，输入作为输出

  • ...

Information

Question 2: Can you summarize how information would be:

  1. Captured

    • Scanner-like device for copying physical paper/images

      用于复印物理纸/图像的类似扫描仪的设备

    • Pen-like device for annotation/input

      用于注释/输入的类似笔的设备

    • Physical insertion of microfilm

      缩微胶卷的物理插入

  2. Organized

    • Microfilm as storage medium. The desired content is mechanically selected and projectedon one of the screens.

      缩微胶卷作为存储介质。所需的内容被机械地选择并投影到其中一个屏幕上

  3. Retrieved

    • Direct selection (via code/index)

      直接选择（通过代码/索引）

    • ”scroll” through information with lever

      用杠杆“滚动”信息

    • Follow trails 跟随小径

Memex and human memory

Question 3: What is the relationship between the Memex and our human memory?

”It operates by association. With one item in its grasp, it snaps instantly to the next that is suggested by the association of thoughts, in accordance with some intricate web of trails carried by the cells of the brain.

“它通过协会运作。掌握一个项目后，它会根据大脑细胞携带的一些复杂的轨迹网，立即捕捉到由思想联想暗示的下一个项目。

It has other characteristics, of course; trails that are not frequently followed are prone to fade, items are not fully permanent, memory is transitory.”

当然，它还有其他特点；不经常遵循的轨迹容易褪色，项目不是完全永久的，记忆是短暂的。”

We notice that older classification methods (e.g. Dewey Decimal Classification, or boxes of punch cards) work hierarchically.

我们注意到旧的分类方法（例如杜威十进制分类或穿孔卡片盒）是分层工作的。

One of the problems there is that content needs to be physically duplicated in order to be in multiple places at the same time (e.g. a book can only be in one library section at the same time).

存在的问题之一是内容需要物理复制才能同时位于多个位置（例如，一本书只能同时位于图书馆的一个部分）。

The Memex differs from this in the way that it works by association, just like the human memory. Content is associated with each other, and content can be associated with many other items without duplication.

Memex 与此的不同之处在于它通过关联工作的方式，就像人类记忆一样。内容彼此关联，并且内容可以与许多其他项目关联而不会重复。

Improvement

Question 4: Is there something that you think is missing in the Memex system and would be useful to add?

  • Search functionality (e.g. by keyword, or search for named trails)

    搜索功能（例如通过关键字，或搜索命名轨迹）

  • Networking, remote sharing of content

    联网、远程共享内容

  • Ownership / copyright of content

    内容的所有权/版权

  • Remove the physical limitation of two screens and replace with single big screen that allows one to lay out content in any way he wants (cfr. windows in modern OS user interfaces).

    消除两个屏幕的物理限制并替换为单个大屏幕，允许一个人以他想要的任何方式布置内容（参见现代操作系统用户界面中的窗口）。

Trial

Question 5: Explain the notion of ’trails’ as described in the Bush paper.

... the basic idea of which is a provision whereby any item may be caused at will to select immediately and automatically another. This is the essential feature of the memex.

……其基本思想是规定，可以随意使任何项目立即自动选择另一个项目。这是 memex 的基本特征。

The process of tying two items together is the important thing. When the user is building a trail, he names it, inserts the name in his code book, and taps it out on his keyboard.

将两个项目联系在一起的过程是重要的事情。当用户构建一条小径trail时，他会为它命名，将名字插入到他的代码簿中，然后在他的键盘上敲出。

Before him are the two items to be joined,projected onto adjacent viewing positions. At the bot- tom of each there are a number of blank code spaces, and a pointer is set to indicate one of these on each item. The user taps a single key, and the items are permanently joined.”

在他面前是要连接的两个项目，投影到相邻的观看位置。在每个项目的底部有许多空白代码空间，并设置了一个指针来指示每个项目上的一个。用户点击一个键，这些项目就会被永久地连接起来。”

”Moreover, when numerous items have been thus joined together to form a trail, they can be reviewed in turn, rapidly or slowly, by deflecting a lever like that used for turning the pages of a book.

此外，当许多项目因此连接在一起形成一个轨迹时，可以通过偏转像用于翻书的杠杆那样快速或缓慢地轮流查看它们。

It is exactly as though the physical items had been gathered together from widely separated sources and bound together to form a new book. It is more than this, for any item can be joined into numerous trails.

此外，当许多项目因此连接在一起形成一个轨迹时，可以通过偏转像用于翻书的杠杆那样快速或缓慢地轮流查看它们。

By associating content we form a mesh of associative trails through our content, linking to get the relevant items. This can be compared to the hyperlinks we all know from the World Wide Web.

通过关联内容，我们通过我们的内容形成了一个关联路径网，链接以获取相关项目。这可以与我们都知道的万维网超链接相提并论。

Realization

Question 6: How close are we to realizing Bush’s vision?

  • Text-To-Speech: The first speech synthesiser was invented in the late 1950’s (very robotic voice, Stephen Hawking). Modern versions are better at generating human sounding speech.
  • Speech-To-Text: Google Assistant, Amazon Echo ,... (not perfect yet)
  • Walnut camera: Google Glass, GoPro with head strap , Microsoft MyLifeBits
  • Interfacing humans with machines: Stephen Hawking had to control his text to speech usinghis remaining functional muscles (cheek), Advanced prostetics
  • Dry Photography: Digital cameras
  • Scanner
  • Memex
    • The paper describes concepts that would later be coined hypertext/hypermedia. Hyper-text eventually led to the World Wide Web, which can be seen as a Memexlike “device”(e.g. linking content through hyperlinks, huge database of information, ...).
    • Wikipedia is also quite related the Memex. Again we see linking of information through hyperlinks, but also a section “related topics”, which is quite close to the trails that Bush envisioned. On top of that, we also see the existence of what Bush calls ”trailblazers”:”There is a new profession of trail blazers, those who find delight in the task of establishing useful trails through the enormous mass of the common record.”.
