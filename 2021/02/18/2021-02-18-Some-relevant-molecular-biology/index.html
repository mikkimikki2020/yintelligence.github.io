Some relevant molecular biology

Website

2021-FEB-11 Video 56:35 ~ end

The cell

  • The fundamental unit of life
  • All cells have very similar internal mechanisms
  • Cells reproduce themselves passing along all the necessary information to reproduce all functionality (heredity)
  • Cells are a bit like computers since they have to respond to all kinds of information: food, threats, ...

There are two big classes: Procaryotes and eukaryotes

All information concerning the functional and structural elements of the cell is encoded in the DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid)

  • Information from one kind of cells can be treated by other kinds of cells
  • DNA is part of a chromosome

DNA contains information to produce thousands of proteins:

  • A gene is a part of a DNA string that encodes a particular protein
  • The genome is the collection of all DNA molecules

A human has 46 DNA molecules in every cell organised in chromosomes.

In procaryotes, like bacteria, there is often one circular DNA molecule

The structure of DNA and RNA 1:06:00

DNA and RNA are linear structures that consist of 4 types of nucleotides.

A nucleotide is constructed of 3 parts (Fig. A) : a base , a sugar and a phosphate group

The difference between the four types is in the base part, which can be either A,T,G or C.

DNA molecules consist of millions of these nucleotides

The beginning of the DNA is annotated with 5’ and the end with 3’

a strand = a sequence of nucleotides

DNA consists of two complementary strands

RNA consist of a single strand

These molecules also assume a 3D form, where complementary parts of the RNA strand can interact through hydrogen bonds

The base-pairing is now between: A-U et C-G (Uracil (U) replaces Thymine (T) in RNA !!)

The central dogma of molecular biology 1:18:42

The genes are translated into proteins in two steps

  • step 1 : transcription
    • Parts of the DNA are copied into shorter RNA strands called messenger RNA (mRNA)
  • step 2 : translation
    • The mRNA is translated by the RNA-polymerase into a sequence of amino acids = proteins

DNA replication 1:19:31

During cell division the two DNA strands are separated

Both strands acts as templates on which the complementary strands are formed

RNAs 1:25:38

Messenger RNA (mRNA)

Transfer RNA (tRNA)

Ribosomal RNA (rRNA)

Transcription 1:27:08

mRNA is a strand that is complementary to the noncoding strand of the DNA

The number of mRNA corresponds to the gene expression level in the cell

Translation 1:32:54

Translation from mRNA to proteins

  • uses transfer RNA (tRNA) to perform the translation

the mRNA is read in blocks of 3 nucleic acids (=codon)

  • 43 possibilities = 64 codons
  • BUT only 20 amino acids

The translation is done by the ribosome

  • The ribosome starts at the 5’ and moves into the direction of the 3’
  • It catches tRNA molecules which can associate with specific codon combinations in the mRNA
  • The amino acids linked to the tRNA, become associated through a peptide bond with the already existing sequence

There is no superposition with the codon

Evolution 1:40:16

The analysis of the different genomes provides information on the evolutionary relationships between the different species

Heredity = transfer of genetic information from the parent to the offspring

When a cell divides, the DNA is copied and divided over the two new cells.

Sometimes this process produces errors (mutations) that can

  • Improve the functionality of the cell = selective advantage
  • Destroy the functionality of the cell = cell dies
  • Does not change the functionality of the cell = selectively neutral

Continuous trial and error allows cells and organisms to evolve

Certain DNA parts are more prone to evolutionary change than others

  • e.g. the non-coding regions in DNA

Yet regions important for proteins function need to be conserved

New genes are build out of old ones in 2 ways

Duplication and divergence 1:45:48

Proteins 1:56:54

The majority of objects inside a cell are proteins

Proteins are

  • the elements that define the structure of a cell
  • The robots that perform almost every function inside the cell

The human genome contains 30000 to 35000 proteins

Structural levels 1:57:19

Primary structure 1:58:16

Like DNA and RNA, proteins are sequences of highly modular building blocks = amino acids

Amino acids 1:59:06

All amino acids (AA) are build out of 3 parts :

  • the central Cα
  • nitrogen group
  • sidechain group

The AA are chained together by peptide bonds

Folding 2:01:12

The possible conformations of the main chain (backbone) are limited

The sequence of amino acids folds in the lowest energy conformation

BUT remember the structure is not static, it breathes !

Binding to another protein or molecule may cause structural changes

The folding of a proteins is orchestrated by three types of interactions between the AA

Tertiary structure 2:06:20

The folded 3D structure is the tertiary structure of a protein

Secondary structure 2:06:47

Analyzing the tertiary structure of proteins has resulted in the discovery of some geometric regularities

helices and strands in the structure are produced by hydrogen bonds between the NH and CO groups

Between 50% and 80% of the residues in a protein can be classified in terms of these regular structures

α helices and β strands 2:07:47

Protein domains 2:10:18

Proteins can also consist of multiple globular

parts = domains

WW domains occur in many different proteins across life 2:11:25

Alignment of WW domains from different proteins 2:13:23
