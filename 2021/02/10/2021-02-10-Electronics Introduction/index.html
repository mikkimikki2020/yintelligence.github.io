Electronics: Introduction

Website

Technological point of view

Definition：

The field of electronics refers to the study, design and use of systems that operate by controlling the flow of electrons (or other charge carriers) in active (semiconductor) and passive devices.

  • Passive components: Limited: dissipate energy and exhibit linear behaviours
  • Active components: Semiconductors; amplify incoming signals. exhibit complex, non-linear behaviour.

The difference between electricity/electrical circuits and electronics is the use of active components.

User’s point of view

Most deal only with information: capturing, transmission, processing, representation, visualization, and storage of information or power.

Designer’s point of view

What are the inputs/outputs? What parameters can be optimized for?

Electronics for computing

Dennard Scaling

  • Power = alpha * CFV 2

    • Alpha - percent time switched
    • C - capacitance
    • F - frequency
    • V - voltage

  • Capacitance is related to area

    As the size of the transistors shrunk, and the voltage was reduced, circuits could operate at higher frequencies at the same power.

  • End of Dennard Scaling

    Dennard Scaling ignored the "leakage current" and "threshold voltage", which establish a baseline of power per transistor.

    • As transistors get smaller, power density increases becuase these do not scale with size
    • These created a "Power Wall" that limited practical processor frequency to around 4GHz since 2006

Computing speed does not scale linearly with the number of cores! 2 x 3 Ghz < 6Ghz

  • Coordination overhead, cache coherency
  • Not all problems lend themselves to (much) parallelization
  • Programming difficulty (harder to reason about, complex dependencies)
Solutions: increased processor complexity

Modern processors are not just “faster” clones of older processor generations

Processors have become much more complex, to the point that it has become nearly impossible for a single person to grasp the entire system.

  • x86-64 ASM (assembly) has essentially become another programming layer
  • High-performance analysis has become statistical

Some notable advances:

  • High core count (“AMD Threadripper” goes up to 64 cores as of 2020)
  • Multi-threading within cores, context switching registers
  • Write-combine on bus
  • Instruction-level parallelism (pipelining, superscalar)
  • Speculative execution (branch prediction)
  • Out-of-order execution
  • Single instruction, multiple data (SIMD)
  • Advanced power management, even within chip regions
  • Complex cache hierarchy

Future of computing

As complexity increases, optimization becomes increasingly important!

  • Performance = Saving energy
  • Tight feedback between software and hardware design
  • Tune algorithms to specific architecture (Big-O analysis alone won’t do)
  • New paradigms (quantum, neuromorphic, FPGA)
  • Robustness and errors in algorithms (loss of determinism), Statistical analysis of run-time

Quantum computing

  • Series of bits are encoded as quantum superpositions of 0 and 1, denoted qubits
  • Myth: quantum computers can perform calculations exponentially faster than classical computers.
  • Fact: they can solve specific problem classes highly efficiently, but not others. (Applications in cryptography, optimization, accurate physical simulations of quantum systems)
  • Prediction: quantum computers will become a specialized unit for certain problem classes, like the GPU. (QPU?)
