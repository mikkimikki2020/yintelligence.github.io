Definition

“Mobile Health (mHealth) is a sub-segment of eHealth and covers medical and public health practice supported by mobile devices. It especially includes the use of mobile communication devices for health and well-being services and information purposes as well as mobile health applications.”

The EU infographic on mHealth

This one has a strong unilateral approach:

  • The patient collects data,
  • the doctor consumes the data

There are other perspectives as well.

Smartphones

  • Defined as having an operating systems capable of hosting medical software or it Internet capable
  • Cloud computing allowed more medical programs of higher complexity to be accessed
  • Majority of adults and 80% of physicians carry a smartphone
  • Many access medical issues on the phone and a minority have at least one medical app and/or receive text messages from a healthcare system

In particular developing countries

  • Large distance between patient and caregiver

  • Shortage of skilled people

  • More smartphones than landlines

  • So mHealth could potentially

    • increase access to healthcare
    • improve ability to diagnose and track diseases
    • Provide timelier, more actionable public health information
    • expand access to ongoing medical education and training for health workers

Benefits of using apps

  • Improve Patient Engagement

    • Complexity, long lines, lack of transparency of quality, etc. discourages patient engagement. Having medical application minimizes complexity. Suppose, you are using a medication reminder application, it knows how many tablets you have taken and when you have to take your next pills.

  • Reduced Healthcare Fraud

    • An increase in the number of patient access to healthcare also results in huge medical cost and frauds. With the help of digital health care system, frauds can minimize around $60 billion annually. Mobile applications are best to trace people and transactions that ultimately minimizes frauds.

  • Instant Healthcare Service

    • Without any wait, mobile technology delivers patients a quick way to access doctor’s advice or treatment information. It is useful for rural areas, where patients haven’t found physicians or limited access to them.

      Moreover, it is also helpful when patients are unable to visit the physicians because of chronic diseases. It enables patients and physicians to decide whether a visit to them is essential or not. Giving better care, checking prescriptions, monitoring improvements, etc. become easy with mobile healthcare app.

Technical background

Database at the server side

  • “A database is an organized collection of data, generally stored and accessed electronically from a computer system.” (Wikipedia)

  • There are different ways for organising data in a database. The most common kind of database is the Relational Database, based on the Relational Model (Codd, IBM, 1970).

  • In the Relation Model, data is structured in tables (relations), with rows and columns.

  • Every row has a unique identifier, a primary key

  • Examples: mysql, postgresql, …

  • Simple python bindings available

Example relational DB structure

staff:

id firstname lastname profession
1 John Doe Physiotherapist
2 Eva Max Surgeon

patient:

id firstname lastname address
1 Marc Brigs A street 12
2 Taylor Jennings B street 77

SQL (structured query language)

SQL is a standard language for storing, manipulating and retrieving data in databases.

Examples

Get all patients with first name Marc

SELECT * from patients WHERE firstname='Marc’

Get the names of all patients who visited a surgeon

SELECT patients.firstname, patients.lastname, cases.date from patients,cases,staff

WHERE patients.id = cases.patient_id AND staff.id = cases.dr_id AND staff.profession='Surgeon'

Relational data model strengths

  • Data model is clear.
  • Data is comosed into atomic units.
  • Data is stored once.
  • Data integrity.
  • Complex join queries are nit that fast.

NoSQL: document databases

Strengths of noSQL

  • Intuitive data model: The documtn model directly maps onto objects in code. Easier to wrk with froma coding perspective

  • Flexible schema: The database struture is not pre-defined, so it is adaptive, flexible, …

  • Distributed and Scalable: Documents are independent “units”, so super easy to distribute over multiple databases etc.
